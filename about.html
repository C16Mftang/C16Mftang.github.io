About me

A Little Biography

I’m currently based in Oxford, UK, pursuing my Ph.D. (or D.Phil., as locals likes calling it) in computational neuroscience at the MRC Brain Network Dynamics Unit, supervised by Prof. Rafal Bogacz. In my science-y life, I am interested in biologically plausible machine learning as a tool to understand how the brain works, as well as an approach to more efficient and powerful artificial intelligence. My current research focus is on predictive coding, a simple but elegant algorithm used to model biological vision, which has been utilized to construct biologically realistic neural network models in recent years due to its Hebbian learning rules. Right now, I am attempting to use predictive coding to model associative memory in the hippocampus, and also to incorporate predictive coding into a framework called free-energy. I’m still exploring the city of Oxford, including the mystical rituals and conventions at St Cross College, of which I am (proudly) a student. I will come back and update more about my spare time at Oxford in the foreseeable future.

Prior to my D.Phil., I spent two years at the University of Chicago, Illinois, doing a master's degree in statistics. I worked with Prof. Yali Amit on bio-plausible self-supervised learning, and Prof. Jason MacLean on Spiking Neural Networks there. UChicago is rigorous, relentless and as the cliché goes, where fun goes to die – as is the city of Chicago. The photo above was taken in winter 2021, when the degree in Chicago dropped below -20 Celsius and the Michigan Lake was frozen (so was I).
